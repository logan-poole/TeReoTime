Maramataka

The Māori lunar calendar - traditional guidance for activities based on moon phases

Understanding Maramataka

The maramataka is more than just a calendar - it's a sophisticated system that connects lunar phases with natural rhythms, energy levels, and appropriate activities. Different iwi (tribes) have variations in their maramataka, but the core principles remain consistent: align activities with natural energy cycles for best results.

Ngā Rā o te Wiki

Days of the week in te reo Māori

About the Seven-Day Week

The seven-day week was introduced to Māori culture through European contact and Christianity. Traditionally, Māori used the maramataka (lunar calendar) to organize time and activities. The adaptation of weekday names shows how te reo Māori evolved to incorporate new concepts while maintaining its linguistic structure.

Ngā Tau Māori

The Māori number system - understanding how numbers work in te reo

The Māori Counting System

The Māori number system is logical and consistent. It uses an additive pattern where larger numbers are built by combining smaller ones with 'mā' (and). For example, 25 is "rua tekau mā rima" (literally "two tens and five").

This same system is used in the Te Reo Time app to tell the time in te reo Māori.

Time-Telling Examples

1:00 - Ko te tahi karaka (It is one o'clock)

2:15 - Ko te tekau mā rima meneti i te rua karaka (15 minutes past 2 o'clock)

3:30 - Ko te toru tekau meneti i te toru karaka (30 minutes past 3 o'clock)

4:45 - Ko te tekau mā rima meneti ki te rima karaka (15 minutes to 5 o'clock)
